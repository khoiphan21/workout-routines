# Mini Routines (Busy-Day Sessions)

Short hypertrophy and skill sessions designed for days when a full workout isn't possible — e.g. long coding days. Each mini routine takes **8–12 minutes** including a light warm-up and covers a specific muscle pairing.

## Rationale

### Why not single scattered sets?

Spreading individual sets throughout the day ("a set here and there") fails to generate meaningful training stimulus for this program because:

- **Proximity to failure matters.** Your program prescribes RPE 8–9 (1–2 RIR) for hypertrophy. A cold, casual set between coding blocks will land at RPE 5–6 — too far from failure to count as a "hard set" (`research/strength-hypertrophy.md`).
- **Warm-up and sequencing exist for a reason.** Heavy power work (weighted dips at 85–90%, pause bench) needs the full warm-up ramp. Cold heavy sets risk injury.
- **Superset structure breaks down.** Doing one exercise now and its pair 45 minutes later eliminates the metabolic and time-efficiency benefits.

### Why mini-sessions of 3 sets work

Three working sets with a light warm-up is the minimum unit that produces a countable training stimulus:

- **Proximity to failure is achievable.** You can ramp to RPE 8–9 across 3 consecutive sets. These count as hard sets.
- **Light warm-up addresses tissue readiness.** One set at ~50% for 8–10 reps is sufficient for moderate cable/bodyweight loads.
- **Volume tracking stays reliable.** Three sets at proper effort = 3 hard sets you can log and count toward your weekly 10–16 set targets.
- **Research supports flexible frequency.** When weekly volume is equated, splitting across more sessions doesn't hurt hypertrophy — it may even help by reducing per-session fatigue (`research/strength-hypertrophy.md`; Pelland et al. 2025 meta-regression).

### What stays in the full session

Power triples (weighted dip, bench, pull-up, chin-up) and high-skill calisthenics (muscle-ups, nordic curls) still need the full warm-up protocol and exercise sequencing. On time-pressed days, do the power block + skills as one continuous 25–30 min session, then scatter hypertrophy minis later.

## Equipment

All mini routines use the same home gym setup as the main program:

- **GM2 cable work:** Pull A/B/C, Push A/B/C exercises
- **Rings:** Ham curl, levers
- **Bar/floor:** L-sit, handstand holds
- **Bands:** Warm-up movements

## How to pick on a busy day

Pick **3–4 minis** that hit different areas across your coding blocks:

| Example day | Mini 1 (morning) | Mini 2 (midday) | Mini 3 (afternoon) | Mini 4 (late) |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Pull focus | Pull A | Pull B | Core | Skill B |
| Push focus | Push A | Push B | Legs A | Core |
| Full body | Pull A | Push B | Legs A | Core |
| Light day | Skill A | Core | Skill B | — |

This gives **6–8 hard sets** per muscle touched — at the minimum effective dose (4–12 sets/muscle/week). On weeks where you also complete 1–2 full sessions, these minis top up volume into the **10–16 hard-set** range the program targets.
