# Exercise Science Research

Learnings from verified exercise science research that can inform program design and progression.

## Purpose

This folder houses summaries and references to peer-reviewed research on strength, hypertrophy, recovery, and programming. Use these findings to ground program decisions in evidence rather than tradition or bro-science.

## Topics covered

### Strength & hypertrophy
- Minimum effective volume (MEV) vs maximum recoverable volume (MRV)
- Set proximity to failure (RIR)
- Training frequency
- Progressive overload
- Stretch-mediated hypertrophy / lengthened partials

### Recovery & adaptation
- Rest intervals between sets
- Recovery between sessions
- Sleep and performance
- Deload protocols

### Exercise selection & technique
- Compound vs isolation
- Exercise order (pre-exhaustion, compound-first)
- Tempo and time under tension
- Range of motion (full vs partial)

### Periodization & programming
- Linear vs undulating periodization
- Concurrent training (strength + cardio interference)
- Warm-up and activation
- Skill acquisition (calisthenics progressions)

### Population-specific
- Beginners vs intermediates
- Aging and muscle
- Sex differences

## How to add research

1. Create a new `.md` file for each topic (e.g. `mev-mrv.md`).
2. Summarize key findings with citations (author, year, journal or source).
3. Note practical implications for this program.
4. Link from this index.

## Sources to prioritize

- Meta-analyses and systematic reviews over single studies.
- Peer-reviewed journals (e.g. *J Strength Cond Res*, *Sports Med*, *Eur J Appl Physiol*).
- Reputable practitioners who cite primary sources (e.g. Schoenfeld, Nuckols, Helms).
