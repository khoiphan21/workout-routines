# Chest-Supported Row (Cable, Gym Monster 2)

Heavy horizontal pull on the **cable stack**—replaces weighted pull-up on [Pull A power](../programs/khoiphan21/push-pull-gym-monster-2/index.md#day-2--pull-a). Uses the **same rigging idea as lat pulldown** (both pulleys → one attachment), but **mid pulley height** and a **chest-supported bench** so you can load the full GM2 stack without kneeling on a lat-pulldown line.

This is **not** a barbell-J-hook / “Smith” setup. The barbell hooks on the vertical posts only **rack a bar**; they do not provide the pulling path for this exercise.

## Pulley setup (read this first)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Where do the cables attach?** | **Both pulleys on the vertical frame bars** (left and right), **not** the low floor platform. |
| **Floor / platform end?** | **No** for this lift. Low anchors are for leg curls, chops, etc.—the pull angle will be wrong for a chest-supported row. |
| **How is this like lat pulldown?** | **Two cables** (one per side) → **one cable row bar** (straight bar on both clips). Same dual-motor idea as a lat bar. |
| **How is it different from lat pulldown?** | **Lower pulley height** + **bench** (chest on pad). You pull **toward your lower chest**, not down from overhead. |

### Height on the vertical bars (levels 0–9)

GM2 cable anchors run **floor (0) → top of frame (9)** on each vertical post ([equipment doc](../equipment/gym-monster-2.md#cable-system)).

**Start here for Pull A power:**

1. Set **left and right pulleys to the same notch**, around **level 4–6** (mid frame).
2. Clip a **cable row bar** to **both** cables (straight bar—**not** two Smart PowerGrip handles).
3. Place **Works Plus** at **~30–45°**, **chest on the pad** (face the pad), feet on the floor.
4. With arms extended, the bar should sit roughly in line with **mid-sternum**.
5. Row to **lower chest / upper abs**. If the pull feels upward into your neck, **lower one notch**; if you’re pulling toward your belt, **raise one notch**.

**Typical reference heights (tune to your arm length):**

| Movement | Pulley height (0–9) | Anchor |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Lat pulldown (kneeling) | **~7–9** (high) | Vertical bars |
| **This exercise** | **~4–6** (mid) | Vertical bars |
| Seated cable row (upright, 90°) | **~3–5** (mid-low) | Vertical bars |
| Leg curl / low work | **~0–2** | Often platform / low |

Always set **left and right to the same level** so load stays even.

## How to perform

- Sit on the bench facing the pad; lean in so **chest stays on the pad** through the set (light hip weight is fine).
- **Pronated or neutral grip** on the row bar; drive **elbows back**; squeeze shoulder blades at the finish.
- Lower until arms are extended without rounding off the pad.
- **3×3** power: **3–4 min** rest; **+2.5 kg** on GM2 after a clean 3×3.

## Why not lat pulldown for power?

Kneeling lat pulldown on GM2 often caps load near **bodyweight** without a knee brace. Chest-supported **cable rows** brace through the bench and load horizontally—same program slot as weighted pull-up, different line of pull.
