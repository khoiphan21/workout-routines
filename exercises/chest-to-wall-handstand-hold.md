# Chest-to-Wall Handstand Hold

Shoulder-stacking and line work facing the wall. Chest stays close to the wall so the body learns **open shoulders** and a straight handstand line—foundation for freestanding work.

## How to perform

- Start standing, place hands on the floor ~15–25 cm from the wall (adjust by limb length).
- Walk feet up the wall until the **chest approaches the wall**, hips open, arms straight.
- Push the floor away; gaze slightly forward; breathe steadily.

## Prescription (Push B)

- **Power week:** 3×20–30 s holds. Rest 2–3 min.
- Shorter holds if form degrades; add time or move hands closer only when line stays clean.

## Progression

- Shorten wall distance as control improves; later reduce wall contact toward freestanding line drills.
