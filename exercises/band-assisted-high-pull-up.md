# Band-assisted High Pull-Up

⚙️ Technique:

- Set a pull-up bar and loop a resistance band over it (or choke one end); place your foot or knee in the band for assistance.
- Hang with a hollow body, initiate with the lats, and pull so the bar travels toward the lower chest or waist—higher than a standard chin-over-bar pull-up.
- Control the descent; avoid excessive kip; match assistance to keep reps crisp.

🎯 Work prescription:

- 3x5 (Power Week Pull A skill pair with front lever hold).
- 60–75 s rest after this move when paired as written in the program.

📈 5 progression levels:

- Level 1: 3x5 with a strong band and full ROM toward the waist.
- Level 2: 3x5 with a medium band; same bar path.
- Level 3: 3x5 with a light band.
- Level 4: 3x5 minimal band or fewer reps with the next-lightest band until 3x5 is clean.
- Level 5: Same pattern without a band (high pull-up) or add a very light load only if form stays strict.

✅ Progress when:

- You can hit the prescribed reps with the same assistance while keeping the bar path high and shoulders happy.

🪜 Scaling tools:

- Thicker or thinner band, or band-only bodyweight pull-ups if high pulls are not yet accessible.
